For example you might describe your process as follows: "Initially the values for $a$, $b$ and $c$ were not very good, $a$ and $b$ were too small, though $c$ seemed good. Finally adjusting these further produced a parabola with a good approximation of the data.."¶


Initially, I set $a,b,c = -3, 2, 0.00001$, even $c$ is pretty close to $0$, and $a$ is negative, $b$ is positive as expected, but it is not pretty good to see this parabola function is downward. Then, I adjusted $a,b,c = -1, 2, 0.001$, this time, $c$ is getting closer to $0$, and keep $b$ be the same, just decrease $a$, which makes clearer to see the downward trend of this parabola function. Finally, I set $a,b,c = -1, 4, -2.5$, this time, I made $c$ as close to $-3$ as possible, keep $b$ be $4$, and assign $-1$ to $a$. Now, this looks a very good approximation of the data. At first since the parabola needs to open downward, a wild guess for $a$ was $-1$. Next it was clear that at $x=0$, the points should be around $-3$, so there the $c$ value was fixed. Next as $b$ takes maxima at poitive value, some positive values form range $2$ to $6$ was tried to get the best one at $4$. Finally the $c$ value was adjusted to $-2.5$ for better result.¶
